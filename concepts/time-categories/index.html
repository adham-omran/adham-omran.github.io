Time Categories

[Adham Omran] · May 6, 2021

Abstract

  • This concept states that we should look at time in terms of its category in addition to its blocks (Deep Work Blocks) and roles (Roles).
  • Simply, time can be seen as categories, the two main ones are Doing and Thinking. Thinking involves activities such as study, research and reading (Reading queue).
  • Doing invovles anything action-oriented such as practice, writing for publishing (NOT note-taking or formulation), recording, composing, coding for a project (NOT reading guides)

Application

  • Use the 80/20 division of Doing/Study
  • I want to use this on a weekly scale, assuming work weeks are 6 days, 12 hours a day (4 a.m. - 4 p.m.) for a total of 72 hours of work, this means that 80% is equivalant to 57.6 hours and 20% is 14.4 hours.
  • Example: Start each work day with 30 minutes of study inside SuperMemo, this sums up to 3 hours (11.4 left), a configuration in testing is to put all these into a single day (Friday) use the remaining 5 days as pure DO days.
