Writing & Production

  • Songwriting Definition
  • Writing a song is the process of writing sections and arranging them to create a musical piece, the sections themselves are formed from elements, these elements can be a drum groove, guitar rhythm loop, or any musical idea that has been written -or improvised- and recorded.
  • After writing a section (verse or chorus) a relative section can be written (e.g. a verse to a chorus) by changing the arrangement and elements of the section.
  • I can write sections with no lead and let my band mates play lead.

Recording

  • Divide recording sessions based on instruments

    • For example, a day for guitar, a day for mixing guitar and tone, a day for vocals, a day for mixing vocals, etc.

  • Separate Recording and Mixing

    • When recording guitar, do it in raw so you can edit the tone later, use a general tone that fits the recording purpose, but don’t be bothered by it being a “perfect” tone.

Workflow

Starting Points

  • Who am I creating this song for?
  • What story do I want to tell?
  • What songs do I like? Can I recreate what I like?
    • Compile from multiple songs (2~3)
  • OPTIONAL: Use the song to learn a single skill, for example tremolo picking for guitar
  • Begin with a template using the data from above

Composing & Recording

These two concepts are different, you can compose something and learn it for the song.

  • When you have fully finished ideas: Recording should be done in instrument based batches, for example: a day for recording guitar, etc.
  • When you are just starting, play around with ideas and record them as takes, then attempt to arrange those ideas into structures.
  • Arranging: Arrange all the pieces of music into a template based on genre and control its energy and flow.
  • Mixing: Mix using the songs that inspired you as reference.
  • Mastering: Master using your ears as well as reference.

TODO Release

  • If the song is to be released as a single, build up hype for it, don’t just throw it into the void, have at least 10 people expecting the song.
  • If the song is part of an album, tease parts of it on social media with an “Album/EP Soon”. Only do this when you have at least three songs being worked on.

General Principles

  • Write with what you have, it’s okay to not rely on your head.
  • You could A/B ideas to people.
  • Do a number of takes, pick the best to your ears.
  • Simplify ideas.
  • Build upon simple ideas.
  • Use the chorus to deliever the core message.
  • Use older projects for ideas.

Questions to ask while writing

  • Could I simplify?

  • Lyrics

    • Am I saying something?
    • Is the storytelling good?
    • Is it dramatic?
    • Is this going somewhere?

  • Music

    • Are the hooks simple enough?
    • Can I make this element stronger?
    • Should I make it weaker?
    • How do my sections connect?
